DOCUMENTARIAN

Nathalie Bibeau is a director and producer of international award-winning productions. She started her career at CBC and Radio-Canada's landmark series, Canada: A People’s History/Canada: une histoire populaire, and has since produced documentary projects such as 8th Fire/8e feu, nominated for the Canadian Screen Awards, and Africa on the Move, which won the Gemini for Best Documentary Series in 2011. Her work has taken her everywhere from the sub-Arctic to Angola and the swamps of Louisiana.

Her latest roles have been as a director for CBC’s Keeping Canada Alive and Keeping Canada Safe, and as a key member of the producing team on the highly-acclaimed feature documentary, Sugar Coated (TVO/ Canal D/ ZDF/Arte), which premiered at Hot Docs 2015, screened at festivals around the world, is currently available on Netflix (US/Canada), was nominated for three Canadian Screen Awards, and won the prestigious Donald Brittain Award for Best Social and Political Documentary. She was also the lead producer on the associated digital media project (TELUS/ TVO/ FMC / University of Toronto) that created a very successful website (120,000 users to date) and mobile application, One Sweet App.

She has written for the Globe and Mail, hosted the Peter Gerretsen Awards at Ryerson University, and volunteered for War Child in Ethiopia. She holds a B.A. from McGill University, an M.A. in Russian History from the University of Toronto, and is fluent in English, French and Swedish.
